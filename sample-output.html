Current-state split

Evidence discipline

Risk handling

Sample pack anatomy

The public sample should mirror the real pack shape safely.

This sequence shows the minimum anatomy of a believable Statepath delivery pack: first-read summary, evidence ledger, truth map, risk notes, and one bounded next-route recommendation.

01

README / first-read summary

Open with the short answer: what the project is, what is real, what is risky, and what the next move should be.

02

Evidence ledger

Show the agreed surfaces, what they can prove, and the supporting evidence structure behind the decision pack.

03

Current-state truth map

Show the legible split between what is confirmed, partial, blocked, or still unresolved.

04

Direct / derived / unresolved labels

Make the confidence status explicit enough that the client can see where the answer is proven and where caution still belongs.

05

Risk and caveat notes

Name the parts that still limit confidence so the recommendation does not overclaim what the evidence can support.

06

Next-route recommendation

End with one bounded recommendation and why it wins over the obvious alternatives.

07

Optional follow-through boundary

If relevant, keep follow-through clearly separate instead of silently implying that ongoing implementation is already included.

Build the public sample from safe fictional, internal non-client, or permission-cleared material only. The point is to show the structure, not to leak real client detail or fake permission.

Redacted preview modules

What a safe public sample actually shows — and what remains hidden.

These four modules are the public proof surface: enough to show evidence discipline, truth labels, risk handling, and route quality without exposing a live client pack.

Redacted preview module

Evidence view

Truth map

Show how the pack separates confirmed, partial, blocked, and unresolved material without leaking implementation-sensitive detail.

  • Shown publicly: the label system, decision thresholds, and the current-state split.
  • Redacted in live work: line-item technical specifics, sensitive screenshots, and internal operating detail.
Redacted preview module

Risk notes

Show the caution structure clearly enough that the sample proves restraint rather than just tidy formatting.

  • Shown publicly: blocker categories, route caveats, and why the next wrong move matters.
  • Redacted in live work: exploitable weaknesses, private commercial detail, and sensitive attributions.
Redacted preview module

Next-route output

Show one bounded recommendation, why it wins, and where optional follow-through begins without pretending the audit already covers implementation.

  • Shown publicly: the winning route shape, main rationale, and the implementation boundary.
  • Redacted in live work: client-specific timelines, ownership calls, and task-level implementation detail.
A credible public sample is stronger because it shows what remains redacted just as clearly as what is shown. That restraint is part of the authority model, not a weakness in the page.

Proof restrictions

Permission and redaction rules are part of the trust model.

Statepath should not imply public permission from private success. The sample must stay safer than the temptation to over-prove.

What may be shown publicly
  • safe fictional, internal non-client, or permission-cleared material
  • anonymous or redacted structural examples
  • the shape of the pack and its decision logic
  • non-identifiable screenshots only if permission is actually recorded
What will not be shown without permission
  • testimonials, quotes, logos, or named-client details
  • identifiable screenshots or confidential exports
  • claims that imply broad implementation capacity the proof does not support
  • public proof created by stretching a private success into a public story
This page shows structure, redaction discipline, and proof restraint. It does not claim public client permission where none is recorded clearly enough to defend.
